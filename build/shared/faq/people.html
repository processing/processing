Who creates Processing?

Ben and Casey started Processing in Fall 2001 and have been co-developing it since. Over the years, a growing number of people have been making additional contributions to the project. The current state of the project wouldn't have been possible without this assistance. We're always looking for people to join in and with the coming release of Processing version 1.0, we hope to distribute the development of the project among a small group of dedicated individuals.

Where is Processing (Affiliations)?

The ideas behind Processing extended from explorations in the Aesthetics and Computation Group (ACG) led by Professor John Maeda at the MIT Media Laboratory from 1996-2003(?). Prof. Maeda's Design By Numbers project is the direct precedent for Processing. The project originated in Fall 2001 at the (ACG) where Ben Fry was a PhD student and the Interaction Design Institute Ivrea in Italy where Casey Reas was a professor. The project is currently directed from the Broad Institute where Ben Fry is a researcher and UCLA's Department of Design | Media Arts where Casey Reas is a professor. The affiliated Wiring project is directed by Hernando Barragan from the Universidad de Los Andes in Bogota.
